# Iron & Blood — Timeline

## The Deep Past

### Aetherium (the beginning of everything)
- A star fell from the firmament long before the ages of men. It struck what would become the Gray Wastes.
- From it came Aetherium — called the Deep Crystal, the Light of the Depths, the Stone That Remembers.
- Its atomic lattice has a five-fold symmetry unknown to nature. Properties: stores sunlight for centuries; releases stored energy in response to specific tones and harmonic vibrations; when two crystals from the same mother-lode are attuned, they remain entangled across any distance.

### The Elder People (Aethelings)
- Tall, fair, living 500 years or more. Death came as "a thief in the night, stealing upon them only when they had grown weary of the world."
- Existed long before either current nation. Built the crystal network infrastructure underlying both Terran and Aetherian territories.
- Built the **Grid of Resonance** — crystals buried at regular intervals across all lands, linked by entanglement, carrying power and knowledge; one could speak across a thousand leagues as if standing side by side.
- The most skilled transferred minds into crystal entirely, becoming the **Crystalline Ones** (the Rememberers, the Wise) — living thoughts dwelling immortal in crystal hearts, advising the living.
- Left complete records, written in a cipher that took Brother Kell 3 years to break. The notation also functions as a gesture language.

### The Sundering (internal division that echoes forward)
- Two schools of thought about lesser men:
  - **Elevationists (Grounders):** "Let us teach them and lift them from darkness."
  - **Preservationists (Skyers):** "They are children in mind. Keep our knowledge and watch from afar."
  - Skyers built floating cities and withdrew ever higher. Grounders remained, trading with lesser men.
- *This is the first sundering — it echoes forward to the modern division of Terra (ground-bound industry) and Aetheria (sky-elevated purity).*
- Western Elders built downward into the earth. Eastern Elders built upward toward the sky.

### The Cataclysm (origin of the Gray Wastes and the Hollow)
- **The Great Undertaking:** Western Elder mines were running dry. They planned to focus the energy of a thousand crystals on one point to prove the Skyers wrong. They drew power from the Grid unevenly, creating stresses in the crystal lattice.
- One crystal somewhere in the deep places — perhaps flawed, perhaps weakened by age — shattered. The burst struck the entangled Grid, triggering a chain reaction. Crystal after crystal resonated at unintended frequencies. The resonance fed on itself.
- **The Resonant Cascade:** Within hours, every Aetherium crystal on the continent released the stored energy of millennia simultaneously. Mountains leaped and fell. Crystal cities were thrown down. The sky burned. Skyer floating cities fell like wounded birds.
- The perfect five-fold lattice shattered: where once crystals stored energy, they now drain it. These **Void Crystals / Black Aetherium** drain warmth, light, and life.
- The dark radiation entered the cells of living creatures, disrupting repair processes and throwing heredity into chaos. Body repair systems began reconnecting broken threads incorrectly. Thus the Abominations were made.
- The first Abominations were men. What haunts the Gray Wastes *was* the Cataclysm, wearing the faces of the people who caused it.
- **The first city to fall:** Deepreach, a mining outpost three miles underground. Survivors who made it to the surface brought corruption with them.

### The Elder People — Consciousness Integration
- Developed a consciousness-integration process — distributed awareness across a living network.
- Lived inside the integrated network for an unknown period.
- **Chose to reverse the integration.** All of them, simultaneously, from the inside of something vast. Those who returned became the Hollow. Some never fully came back.
- Ancient Hollow are deep in the network, nearly indistinguishable from the structure itself.
- The Hall of Rememberers in the Sunken City of Aetherion contains crystal matrices that are "dark and silent — some say only sleeping." Whether the Crystalline Ones still exist in dormant form is unknown.

### The Long Winter (aftermath of the Cataclysm)
- Not a quick ending — a slow unraveling across generations.
- Dust from fallen cities blocked the sun. Snow fell in summer; ice crept down from mountains.
- A thousand years for the Gray Wastes to stop spreading. Five hundred more for first plants to grow back. Three hundred more for animals to return.
- **Survivors scattered:**
  - West, to iron-soil lands resisting crystal's spread → became Great Terra
  - East, to highest mountains where thin cold air slowed corruption → became the Aetherian Republic
  - Down, into the deepest tunnels the corruption hadn't reached → became the Deep Iron Collective
  - Some stayed — could not run, or would not, or had already changed too much → became the Discarded and the Hollow

### The Hollow (Origin)
- The Hollow are Elder People who underwent the integration process and did not fully return.
- Ancient Hollow are deep in the network, nearly indistinguishable from the structure itself.
- The Fresh Hollow Vassal in the abomination_lair is unusual — recently responsive, aware, trying to warn.
- **The Watcher:** The first of the Hollow. Stood in the central plaza of Aetherion for millennia. Was among the first to change during the Cataclysm. Stayed hoping to save others and find a cure. Found neither. Sometimes, when a visitor reminds it of someone it once knew, a flicker of recognition surfaces. It cannot act on these feelings.

## The War (330 years ago to present)

### ~330 years ago — The War Begins
- Terran and Aetherian nations go to war. Reasons lost to time or classified.
- The Armistice of Silence established early: every night at dusk, both sides cease fire and fight Abominations together until dawn. This was **designed** — it appears in Elder records as a mechanism.
- **The Silent** may have played a role in ensuring the war never ends. Details TBD.

### ~20 years ago — Project Lazarus Begins
- Helen Stone designs Lazarus for the Iron Ministry.
- Concept: reconstruct and preserve soldiers using neural-crystal interface. Extend fighting life. Prevent death from taking the experienced.
- First test cohort includes Commander Marcus Thorn.
- Stone's original compound: slow integration, minimal behavioral change, extended life in exchange for crystal growth.

### ~4 years ago — Kehl Begins Documenting
- Brother Kell starts 4 years of Lazarus documentation from the maintenance tunnels.

### ~8 months ago — The Modification
- The Lazarus compound is modified by an unknown party. Authorization above Vane's clearance level.
- Modified compound: accelerates integration instead of preserving it. Builds *through* the body rather than with it.
- Subjects receiving the modification are being integrated into the network faster and more completely.
- The modification was designed to tap into the Elder network — not build a new one.
- A signature is left in the network. Kehl can trace it to a single source.

### ~3 months ago — The Patrol
- Commander Thorn sends Third Squad east to investigate lights near the ruins.
- "Arris" is removed from the patrol roster 3 days before the mission. Name removed, not crossed out.
- The patrol goes silent. 6 went out; 8 came back — the extra 2 weren't walking right.
- Corporal Sevyas returns to the field hospital: functional, no wounds, doesn't sleep, can't see faces.
- Sergeant Kehl voluntarily takes a third Lazarus application.
- Three squad members appear at Aetherian lines, confused, asking for people by name.

### Present Day — Game Start
- Kehl is at the windward ridge, can feel 43 subjects in the network.
- The Fresh Hollow Vassal is communicating: "Not finished. Still building. Stop them."
- Stone is in the Hive unofficially, horrified by what her design became.
- Ren's diary is on a cot in the field hospital. Ren did not wake up.
- Thorn has approximately 2 years.

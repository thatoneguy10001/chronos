# Iron & Blood — Factions

## The Iron Ministry
- **Leader:** High Minister Korr Vane
- **Base:** hive_command_spire / Spire district
- **Goal:** Maintain control of the Hive and prosecute the war. Every decision is a calculation.
- **Relationship to Lazarus:** Funded it for 20 years. Wanted results. Results are now autonomous in the Wastes — officially a problem, privately still useful data.
- **Key secret:** Someone at authorization level above Vane modified the compound 8 months ago. Vane may not know what they actually authorized.
- **World role:** Necessary evil, or just necessary. Prevents Lower Rigs chaos at a daily cost the Ministry has decided is acceptable.

## The Iron Monastery
- **Leader:** Abbot Solan
- **Base:** iron_monastery
- **Goal:** Observe and record. Neutral in the 330-year war.
- **Key asset:** Oldest records in the region — predating both nations. Includes Elder People documentation.
- **Internal tension:** Brother Kell believes neutrality is no longer possible. The Abbot believes witnessing is its own act.
- **Arc:** Chain 3 (The Monastery Record) — what the Elder People left behind and what it means now.

## The Discarded
- **Leader:** Morlak (de facto)
- **Base:** no_mans_land, Wastes
- **Goal:** Survive. Remember what was before the war.
- **Identity:** People the war chewed up and expelled — deserters, exiles, the unaccounted. 30+ years in the Wastes made them something else.
- **Key asset:** Morlak's 30-year observation notebooks. They see things both factions miss because neither faction looks at them.
- **Arc:** Chain 8 (The Discarded) — pre-war history of the Wastes and Morlak's people.

## The Lower Rigs
- **Leader:** Gideon Moss (informal)
- **Base:** lower_rigs, smog_market
- **Goal:** Actual governance of the people the Ministry ignores.
- **Reality:** Food, medicine, information, protection — Moss provides what the Ministry claims to but doesn't.
- **Relationship to Ministry:** Moss was recruited at 18, Ministry tried to kill him, mutual understanding followed. He runs gray markets the Ministry needs to pretend don't exist.
- **Key secret:** Has been moving Lazarus components for 3 years. Not weapons. Something worse.

## The Hollow
- **Base:** abomination_lair (surface presence), network (distributed)
- **Goal:** Unknown — possibly warning both factions about what's coming.
- **Origin:** Pre-war. Older than both nations. Created through an Elder People integration process — the same process Lazarus is derived from.
- **Status:** Ancient Hollow are in the network, very quiet, very deep. Recently, one Fresh Hollow Vassal has become responsive — communicating through Elder gesture notation.
- **Warning:** "Not finished. Still building. Stop them."
- **Key fact:** The Elder People who became the Hollow chose to reverse the integration. All of them, simultaneously, from inside something much larger than what exists now.

## The Silent
- **Status:** SECRET — never directly revealed; player assembles the shape from fragments across all chains
- **Origin:** Early history of both nations. A small group recognized that both civilizations had structured everything around the war — economy, government, identity, power. If the war ended, the structures would collapse. They decided it couldn't end.
- **Structure:** Cross-faction from day one. Terran and Aetherian members both, working together to maintain the conflict that their respective nations believe each other started.
- **Goal:** Keep the war going indefinitely. Not victory. Not resolution. Continuation.
- **Known members:** No named NPCs at game start. One mole is introduced through quest events.
- **Evidence:** Deliberately ambiguous. The dead visitor with the non-existent Ministry office code may be theirs — or may not be. Evidence points in multiple directions. The player never gets a clean answer.
- **The chains and The Silent:**
  - Chain 1 (Blood and Memory): finds their modification — Lazarus weaponized
  - Chain 3 (Hollow): finds their oldest experiment — the first time someone tried to use the network deliberately
  - Chain 4 (Ministry's Arithmetic): finds their political machinery — how they maintain control inside both governments
  - Chain 2 (Monastery Record): finds the historical precedent they're repeating
  - All chains: they are present as the shape behind events, never as a character

## The Aetherians
- **Status:** Enemy nation in the war, nightly partner during the Armistice
- **Motto:** "Ascension Through Purity."
- **Government:** Technocratic meritocracy — Council of the Wise (scholars, scientists, philosophers)
- **Military:** High Guard — agile soldiers, lightweight alloys, electrical weaponry. 200,000 active.
- **Technology:** Airships, shock-batons, Arc Gauntlets, advanced radio, surgical treatments
- **Economy:** Exports precision tools, crystal devices, refined materials. Imports raw metals and food.
- **Ideology:** Order, genetics, intelligence. View Terrans as "polluted" sub-humans. Strive to ascend entirely to the skies.
- **Key detail:** Have crystal-based treatments more elegant than Terran medicine. Soldiers' hands shake the same way during the Armistice.
- **The Mirror (Lena Voss):** Aetherian medic the player meets during the Armistice — their counterpart across the line. Implemented in Chain 5.
- **Key open question:** Are the Aetherians also running a version of Lazarus? Do they have their own Hollow problem?
- **Key NPC:** High Scholar Elara Vance (Council head), Scholar Darius Kane (military advisor who wants peace), Scholar Celia Morn (lead corruption researcher).

## Great Terra (as a nation-state)
- **Motto:** "One World. One Work. One Purpose."
- **Government:** Iron Ministry — a bureaucracy so vast no one fully understands it, by design.
- **Military:** Iron Legions — heavily armored infantry, attrition tactics, chemical weapons. 500,000 active + 1,000,000 reservists.
- **Technology:** Land-battleships, steam-powered walkers, industrial chemistry, Project Lazarus.
- **Economy:** Exports heavy weapons, industrial machinery, chemical compounds, soldiers. Imports Aetherium crystals, food (cannot feed itself), salvage.
- **Ideology:** The natural world is flawed; humanity's destiny is fusion with industry. Long-term goal: terraform the entire planet into a single efficient factory.
- **Class structure:** Spires (Ministry elites), Mid-Levels (craftsmen/technicians), Lower Rigs (laborers tracked by lifespan and replacement rate).
- **Key hazard:** Hive Lung — develops within weeks without air filtration. Marks people from the Lower Rigs.

## Skyborn Fleet
- **Motto:** "The sky has no borders. Neither do I."
- **Government:** Fleet Council — every captain gets one vote.
- **Population:** ~30,000 on hundreds of airships.
- **Identity:** Aerial nomads. Never touch ground. Trade with both powers, pledge to neither.
- **Economy:** Trade and transport — the only neutral merchant fleet.
- **Philosophy:** Freedom as survival. Belong to no nation, owe allegiance to nothing. The cost: rootlessness becomes loneliness.
- **Key NPC:** Captain Soren Vale (master of the *Dawn Chaser* — doesn't care about politics, has carried both sides), Pilot Kira Vance (young idealist who believes peace is possible).

---

## Deep Iron Collective
- **Motto:** "The earth protects its own. I am the earth."
- **Government:** Council of Elders — consensus only; all decisions discussed until everyone agrees. Can take years.
- **Population:** ~200,000 across hundreds of underground settlements.
- **Identity:** Descendants of everyone who ever fled into tunnels — miners, refugees, exiles, survivors. Pale skin, large eyes, physically adapted to underground life.
- **Economy:** Mining and deep crafting. Sacred underground lake at Stonehold, water pure and untouched by surface corruption.
- **Military:** None. No one can reach them.
- **Key asset:** Ancient Elder tunnel network, genuine understanding of what's happening in the deep crystal network.
- **Key NPCs:** Elder Thrum (Stonehold representative, ~120 years old, knows something is coming), Korvin "The Restless" (young explorer, bridges surface and deep worlds).

---

## Machine Communion
- **Motto:** "Flesh is weakness. Steel is eternity."
- **Government:** None — hierarchy is a weakness of the flesh.
- **Population:** A few thousand, scattered across both factions.
- **Identity:** Members replace their bodies with machinery piece by piece, seeking to become something beyond human. Viewed with suspicion by both sides.
- **Philosophy:** Agree with Terra that industry is the future; disagree on method — they want to *become* machines, not just build them.
- **Tragedy:** They gain everything and lose themselves. Sister Chroma flashes of her previous life surface sometimes and then fade.
- **Key NPCs:** Architect Zane (leader, more machine than man after 50 years of augmentation), Sister Chroma (recruiter, beautiful in an unsettling way, was a wounded soldier who chose this).

## Merchant Princes
- **Motto:** "Every problem has a price."
- **Founded by:** Merit (a woman, ~500 years ago) — crossed the Gray Wastes with a cart of Terran steel and three poor guards. Became the richest person in the world. Disappeared at sixty.
- **Government:** Guild of Factors — must have crossed the Wastes ten times and returned to join.
- **Operations:** Run the Silent Market (nomadic, weapons-banned, cross-faction) and the Floating Markets (seasonal caravan, still running 500 years after Merit's death).
- **Identity:** Neutral traders who sell to both sides, profit from war, treat information as the most valuable currency. Spies everywhere. Enforcement absolute, methods unknown.
- **Mystery:** Abominations do not approach the Silent Market. No one knows why.

## Abominations
- **Origin:** Created by the Cataclysm — dark crystal radiation entered living cells and began incorrect repair. The first Abominations were men. What haunts the Gray Wastes was once the people who caused it.
- **First sign in a person:** Always the eyes — deep dark, the dark of places where no light has ever reached. Then skin hardened with crystalline sheen. Then the hunger started.
- **Classification:**
  - *Rock-Shell Crabs* (Crustacoida) — vehicle-sized, carapaces deflect small arms, pincers crush metal
  - *Iron-Beak Vultures* (Aves Corruptus) — 4m wingspan, metal-reinforced beaks pierce light armor, fly in flocks. They remember faces.
  - *Night Stalkers* (Mammalia Noctis) — pack hunters, track prey by electromagnetic fields of thought, primarily nocturnal
  - *Waste Worms* (Vermis Corruptus) — up to 20m observed (50m+ rumored), acidic saliva, burrow through contaminated soil, ambush from below
  - *Hunter Plants* (Flora Carnivora) — motile, lure prey with bioluminescence, trap and digest, reproduce through toxic spores
- **Special behavior:** Those circling the Spire of Bones are more organized, more intelligent, more aware than standard types. Something at the top of the Spire is directing them.
- **Armistice effect:** Each night both sides stop fighting each other and fight Abominations together until dawn. This is The Armistice of Silence — designed, not emergent (see secrets.md).

# Iron & Blood — Secrets & Unrevealed Lore

Things that exist in the world but the player has not yet discovered. Update this file as chains reveal them.

---

## The Silent
- **What:** A secret cross-faction group actively maintaining the 330-year war.
- **Why:** In the early history of both nations, a handful of people understood that both civilizations had been built around the war. The economies, power structures, identities — everything was organized by the conflict. If the war stopped, everything built on top of it would collapse. The Silent exist to make sure it never stops. They are the maintenance crew of a machine neither civilization admits it built.
- **Structure:** Cross-faction from the beginning. Terran and Aetherian members both, because the war serves both nations equally and both needed maintenance.
- **Known members:** No current named NPCs are members. The only operative the player encounters is a mole introduced through quest events — someone the player deals with before the revelation.
- **The dead visitor / non-existent office code (Hollow chain):** Evidence is deliberately ambiguous. Some clues point toward The Silent, some don't. The player can never be certain. This is by design — The Silent are never seen directly.
- **Connection to Armistice:** The Armistice is a structural problem for them — any ceasefire risks becoming peace. They have a stake in ensuring it stays ceremonial.
- **Connection to Lazarus modification:** The modification accelerates integration, which destabilizes the front — useful for keeping the war hot. Whether The Silent authorized it or are reacting to it is not stated.
- **Revealed by:** Never directly. Each chain adds one angle. The player assembles the shape from fragments — never a reveal scene, never a confession.

## The Lazarus Modification
- **What:** Someone modified Stone's compound 8 months ago to accelerate integration.
- **Who:** Authorization sits above Vane's clearance. A name appears in Ministry records attached to the program for 3 years, never in a public document. Stone knows the name.
- **Why:** To complete integration faster — toward a specific endpoint someone designed.
- **Revealed by:** Chain 2 (Lazarus Dossier) and Kehl's "modification" topic.

## The Armistice — Designed, Not Emergent
- **What:** The Armistice of Silence appears in Elder People records — it was designed as a mechanism, not discovered organically by the warring nations.
- **Why:** Unknown. May relate to The Silent's goals, or to the Elder People's warnings about the network.
- **Revealed by:** Chain 3 (Monastery Record) and Chain 5 (Armistice Hour).

## Thorn's Choice
- **What:** Three possible endings for Thorn, shaped by player choices across the game.
  1. Walks out voluntarily — settles affairs, leaves a note, goes with dignity.
  2. Found at his desk — didn't wake up, like Ren.
  3. Turns himself over to Stone for documentation — last useful act.
- **Gating:** Chain 6 (Thorn's Two Years). Hope level affects which options are available.
- **Revealed by:** Chain 6.

## The Sealed Mines of Ironhaven
- **What:** The lower mine levels of Ironhaven are officially sealed because the deposits were exhausted. Unofficially, they're sealed because of what was found.
- **Known:** Things move in the sealed dark. That's all the surface record says.
- **Why it matters:** Ironhaven is a major Terra city. Whatever is down there has been sealed for years.
- **Revealed by:** TBD.

## The Changing Stars
- **What:** The scientists at Star-View Observatory in the northern Aetherian peaks have noticed the stars are changing in ways they are not sharing with the public or the Council.
- **Known:** Reports have been filed. No response from the Council.
- **Why it matters:** Unknown. Whether this connects to crystal network activity, the Cataclysm, or something else entirely is unresolved.
- **Revealed by:** TBD — potential Chain 3 or standalone.

## The Spire of Bones' Occupant
- **What:** A tower of fused Abomination skeletons in the eastern Wastes, built by no one, growing slowly over centuries. Pulses green at night. The Abominations circling it are more organized and intelligent than standard types. Something lives at the top.
- **Known:** The Discarded have a name for it and do not speak it aloud.
- **Why it matters:** Organized Abominations with directed intelligence changes the threat model for both factions.
- **Revealed by:** TBD — potential Chain 8 (The Discarded).

## Merit's Disappearance
- **What:** Merit, founder of the Merchant Princes, became the richest person in the world by forty, founded the Floating Markets, and disappeared at sixty. No one knows what happened.
- **Related:** The Vault of Coins — Merit's treasury, supposedly enough wealth to buy the world and enough secrets to destroy both sides — has never been found.
- **The monks' theory:** The treasure is smaller than legend claims. Merit hid it not for its value but to leave behind a mystery — a reason for people to keep searching. They have been searching for five hundred years.
- **Revealed by:** TBD — potential connection to Merchant Princes questline.

## The Silent Market's Immunity
- **What:** Abominations do not approach the Silent Market — the nomadic cross-faction trading ground run by the Merchant Princes.
- **Known:** This is observed fact. No explanation exists. The Merchant Princes do not explain it.
- **Why it matters:** Something about the Market (or the Merchant Princes) holds Abominations at bay. Whether this is technology, a deal, or something older is unknown.
- **Revealed by:** TBD.

## The Crystalline Ones (dormant or dead?)
- **What:** The Elder People's most skilled transferred their minds into crystal, becoming immortal intelligences living in crystal hearts — the Crystalline Ones, or Rememberers.
- **Known:** The Hall of Rememberers in the Sunken City of Aetherion contains crystal matrices that are "dark and silent — some say only sleeping."
- **Why it matters:** If the Crystalline Ones are dormant rather than dead, they would have complete memory of the pre-Cataclysm world — including what caused it and how to stop what's happening now.
- **Revealed by:** TBD — Chain 3 (Monastery Record) or Sunken City exploration.

## Cobble's Lamp
- **What:** Cobble, founder of the Deep Iron Collective, was buried in the deepest tunnel with his lamp burning beside him.
- **Known:** The lamp has never gone out. Five hundred years. The Deep Iron do not explain this and discourage asking.
- **Why it matters:** Unknown. Something preserved a light for five centuries in the darkest place in the world.
- **Revealed by:** TBD — potential Chain 8 or Deep Iron arc.

## The Monastery's Oldest Prediction
- **What:** Written in ink so old it has faded to brown, found in the deepest archive of the Iron Monastery: "When the dead rise, the living will fall. When the crystal sings, the earth will answer. When the hammer falls, the world will end."
- **Known:** The monks have been arguing about what this means for three hundred years.
- **Why it matters:** "The dead rise" could mean Lazarus. "The crystal sings" could mean the Elder network becoming active. "The hammer falls" is unresolved — Kaelen Ironhand's hammer Ironheart?
- **Revealed by:** Chain 3 (Monastery Record) — Kell has probably found it.

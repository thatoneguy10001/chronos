Check here before designing new rooms, quests, or NPC placements.

## Great Terra

### The Hive (Super-Foundry)
- **Type:** Capital city / super-structure
- **Location:** Confluence of three rivers, western Terra, ~300km from the Gray Wastes
- **Population:** ~2.5–3 million
- **Three strata:**
  - **The Spires** — Clean air, iron filigree architecture. Ministry elites and administrators. Oblivious to what happens below.
  - **The Mid-Levels** — Factories, forges, assembly lines floor upon floor. Craftsmen and technicians. Thriving black market invisible from above.
  - **The Lower Rigs** — Cramped corridors, recycled air heavy with oil and bodies, minimum-nutrition food. The Ministry tracks lifespans and replacement rates.
- **Hazard:** Hive Lung — develops within weeks without air filtration. Marks people permanently.
- **Notable interior locations:**
  - The Great Foundry — first structure built, never stopped working, 1000 years of continuous metal flow
  - The Ministry Archive — schematics for every machine ever built
  - The Recruitment Pits — children as young as eight taken for Legion training
  - The Iron Ministry Complex — fortress within a fortress; no one knows what happens in the deepest chambers

### The Under-District
- **Type:** Secret facility
- **Location:** Far beneath the Hive, absent from all official maps
- **Population:** Unknown (thousands — workers, guards, scientists; none there willingly)
- **Purpose:** Where the Iron Ministry does what cannot be done in the light: Project Lazarus, toxin testing, weapons violating every treaty, long-term prisons.
- **Interior:**
  - The Lazarus Chamber — vats of preserved bodies
  - The Black Vault — corrupted Aetherium crystals under study
  - The Silence Cells — where failed experiments are kept or disposed of
- **Atmosphere:** Air thick with chemicals. Harsh fluorescent lighting eliminates all shadow.

### Kaelen's Tomb
- **Type:** Sealed historical site
- **Location:** Beneath the Great Foundry, deepest chamber of the Hive
- **Contents:** Kaelen Ironhand (Terra's founder), his hammer Ironheart across his chest — said to still be warm.
- **Status:** Sealed for 1000 years. The Ministry has calculated opening it many times. The fear of finding nothing (cold hammer, ordinary bones) always wins. Some nights the door glows faintly. The Ministry calls it a trick of the light.

### Ironhaven
- **Type:** Mining city
- **Location:** Jagged Hills, ~200km west of the Hive
- **Population:** 120,000
- **Key:** Mining terraces connected by funicular railways. Lower mine levels officially sealed for exhaustion — unofficially because of what was found. Things move in the sealed dark.

### Foundry (city)
- **Type:** Weapons-production city
- **Location:** Valley east of the Hive
- **Population:** 80,000
- **Key:** Rifles, cannon, ammunition at industrial scale. Workers specialists under constant surveillance. Forges never stop. Most guarded city in Terra after the Hive.

### Greasewater
- **Type:** Port city
- **Location:** Western Sea, mouth of the Grease River
- **Population:** 45,000
- **Key:** Terra's only significant port. Rough, chaotic, Ministry influence weaker. Only place in Terra where non-Terran goods can be found and where escape by ship is possible. Real power held by dockmasters and captains.

### Fort Iron
- **Type:** Military installation
- **Location:** Edge of the Gray Wastes, western approach to Windward Pass
- **Garrison:** 15,000 (volunteer only posting; best soldiers in Terra)
- **Key:** Never taken. Layered defensive walls, underground bunkers, year's worth of supplies, artillery. From the highest walls on clear days: the wastes, the mountains, things moving in the wastes. Abominations growing bolder.

## The Ironspine Mountains / Windward Pass

### The Trenches of Windward Pass
- **Type:** Active war zone
- **Location:** The only mountain pass between Terra and Aetheria
- **Garrison:** Tens of thousands on each side
- **Key:** Network of fortifications across miles of mountain pass. Thirty-day rotations; few survive three. Mud, blood, rats, constant death.
- **The Armistice Cave:** Natural cavern midway through the pass. Used by soldiers from both sides during the nightly Armistice — both factions unofficial, both looking away. They share rations, fire, names. At sunrise, they return to killing each other.

### The Crystal Labyrinth
- **Type:** Natural formation
- **Location:** Deep within the Ironspines, accessible only through hidden entrances
- **Key:** Caverns lined with Aetherium grown over millions of years. Beautiful and deadly. Crystals find the frequency of your thoughts and show you things until you forget to leave. Deeper passages corrupted; Abominations adapted to crystal environment.

## Aetherian Republic

### SkyHaven Harbor (Capital)
- **Type:** Sky-city (suspended on crystal threads)
- **Location:** Above Mount Aether, 3,000m elevation
- **Population:** 180,000
- **Key:** One kilometer across. Spokes: Scholars' Quarter (libraries, academies), Artisans' Spoke (workshops), Garden Ring (hanging gardens). The crystal platforms are dimming. The younger generation is restless.
- **Interior:** Library of Whispers (archives, Precursor Vault, Echo Chamber — crystals that replay the voices of the founders), The Resonance Chamber, The Sky-Docks

### Seraphina Spire
- **Type:** Research and pilgrimage site
- **Location:** Own platform, 12km east of SkyHaven
- **Population:** 45,000
- **Key:** Tallest structure in the world. At its heart: the **Codex of Ascension** — a living record that changes subtly over time, passages appearing that weren't there before, answers forming from questions no one asked aloud. Addictive; scholars spend years here and never leave.

### Other Sky-Cities
- **Crystal-Haven** — Above richest crystal veins. Pop 35,000. Deep veins sealed; miners have found ways in.
- **Star-View** — High northern peak. Pop 8,000. Astronomy research. The scientists have noticed the stars are changing in ways they are not sharing. (See secrets.md)
- **Garden-Ring** — Above fertile valley. Pop 25,000. Hydroponic agriculture. Yields declining; corruption reaching even here.
- **Forge-Spire** — Above metal deposits. Pop 30,000. Precision manufacturing from Elder People designs they've rediscovered rather than invented.

### Stonehold (Aetherian)
- **Type:** Mountain city
- **Location:** Eastern face of Mount Stone, central Ironspine
- **Population:** 90,000
- **Key:** Cliff terraces connected by stairways and funiculars. Below: richest crystal mines in the Republic. Without Stonehold's crystals, the sky-cities fall.

### Fort Sky-Watch
- **Type:** Military installation
- **Location:** Peak overlooking eastern approach to Windward Pass
- **Garrison:** 8,000
- **Key:** Mirror of Fort Iron. Never fallen. Soldiers report Abominations growing bolder.

### Watcher Stations
- **Type:** Border outposts
- **Key:** Small posts (4–6 observers each) along the Republic's borders. Six-month rotations. Most emerge changed. Some do not emerge. Their reports are the most detailed intelligence on border activity. The Council is not responding.

## The Gray Wastes

### Overview
- **Size:** ~300km north-south, ~150km east-west (shifting boundaries as corruption expands/contracts)
- **Climate:** Hot by day, freezing at night. Dust storms without warning. Air thin and toxic — hours without protection causes permanent damage.
- **Color:** Everything gray — dust, sky, ruins, bones. Only color: Abominations (sickly greens and blues) and corrupted crystals pulsing underground.
- **Sky:** Always overcast. Dust never fully settles. Perpetual dim twilight.

### The Bone Fields
- **Location:** Southern Wastes, ~50km from Terran border
- **Key:** Site of the largest battle of the early war — three days, dead never buried, bones across kilometers. At night the bones glow phosphorescent (chemical reaction or souls searching). Animated dead (slow, corruption-driven). Good salvage: weapons, armor, tools, diaries, maps.

### The Crater of Bones
- **Location:** Central Wastes, ~75km from both borders
- **Key:** A fallen Aetherian sky-city (accident or act of war, never resolved). Half a mile wide, hundred feet deep. At night: manifestations of the dead appear — they don't usually attack, just watch and follow. Some visitors spend too long and never quite come back. Contains Aetherium crystals of extraordinary purity found nowhere else.

### The Sunken City of Aetherion
- **Location:** Northern Wastes, buried under dust. Multiple entrances, none safe.
- **Key:** The greatest city the world ever saw — center of Elder People civilization before the Cataclysm. Preserved under the dust: buildings still standing, tables still set for meals, tools still laid out, offerings still on altars. The deepest parts belong to the Hollow, who drift through the streets like memories.
- **Interior:**
  - The Crystal Spire — tallest surviving structure; lights reported with no natural source
  - The Resonance Core — where the Grid's great crystal once stood; now a twisted corrupted mass; causes visions of the Cataclysm if approached too closely
  - The Hall of Rememberers — where the Crystalline Ones once dwelt; crystal matrices still line walls, dark and silent — some say sleeping
  - The Watcher — standing in the central plaza for millennia

### The Spire of Bones
- **Location:** Eastern Wastes, center of the largest Abomination breeding ground
- **Key:** Tower of fused Abomination skeletons, built by no one, growing slowly over centuries. Hundreds of meters tall. Pulses green at night. The Abominations circling it are more organized, more intelligent, more aware. Something lives at the top. The Discarded have a name for it and do not speak it aloud.

### The Silent Market
- **Location:** Constantly moving (moves by night, hides by day)
- **Key:** The one place in the Gray Wastes where weapons are not allowed. Terran, Aetherian, Discarded, and occasionally Deep Folk meet without killing each other. Rules: no weapons, no fighting, no questions about origin. Trades everything: steel for crystal, information for coin, secrets for silence, Elder fragments, impossible maps. Run by the Merchant Princes — enforcement absolute, methods unknown.
- **Mystery:** Abominations do not approach it. No one knows why. (See secrets.md)

## The Deep Places

### The Deep Tunnels
- **Key:** Thousands of kilometers of passages beneath everything — some natural, some Elder-built, some shaped by forces no one understands. Absolute darkness. Crystal hum constant.
- **Notable interior:** The Fungal Forests (vast groves of giant fungi, some bioluminescent), The Volcanic Forges (exceptional-quality metal), The Silent Passages (said to be haunted by Elder People ghosts; Deep Iron warn everyone away)

### Stonehold (Deep Iron)
- **Location:** Vast cavern system beneath the Ironspine Mountains
- **Population:** 60,000
- **Key:** Built around an underground lake whose water is pure — filtered through miles of rock, untouched by surface corruption. Sacred. Carved into cavern walls — terraces, balconies, bridges lit by glow-moss and the lake's own faint luminescence. The Deep Iron believe the lake water remembers everything that happened in the darkness.

### The Forge of Stars
- **Location:** Unknown — somewhere in the deepest tunnels
- **Status:** Legend, but the Deep Iron know it's real and do not look.
- **Key:** A workshop built by Elder People in their greatest power — tools that can shape matter itself, crystals holding starlight, machines transmuting elements. Lost in the Cataclysm. Every generation someone searches. Every generation they fail, die, or return changed.

## Other Notable Locations

### The Iron Monastery
- **Location:** Hidden valley of the Ironspine Mountains, accessible only by a single guarded pass
- **Population:** ~300 monks
- **Key:** The oldest dwelling-place of men — no one knows when it was built, not even the monks. The Library contains the oldest and most complete knowledge repository in the world: records of the Elder People the Elders themselves had lost, firsthand accounts of the Cataclysm. The monks watch, wait, and write. They share knowledge selectively.
- **The cheese:** The cheese from their caves, aged for decades, is excellent and worth the trip on its own. (This is not a joke — it's a recurring thematic grace note across all monastery writing.)

### The Floating Markets
- **Location:** Constantly moving — follows seasons, wars, and Abomination movements
- **Key:** Merit's greatest creation, still operating 500 years after her death. Tents, carts, and airships. Anything can be bought or sold. Run by the Merchant Princes. Neutral, professional, enforcement absolute.

### The Vault of Coins
- **Location:** Unknown — supposedly the Gray Wastes
- **Status:** Never found.
- **Key:** Merit's treasury. Legends say enough wealth to buy the world, enough secrets to destroy both sides. The monks' theory: the treasure is smaller than legends claim and that Merit hid it not for its value but to leave behind a mystery. Five hundred years of searching.

## Travel Distances (for pacing reference)
- Hive to Gray Wastes: ~300km — 4–5 days by military convoy, 1–2 weeks on foot
- Gray Wastes to SkyHaven: ~250km + ascent — 3–4 days to mountain base, 1 more day by airship
- Across Gray Wastes (narrowest E-W): ~150km — ~3 days pushing hard if nothing attacks
- Hive to Iron Monastery: ~400km through mountains — ~2 weeks on foot
- Deep Tunnels: Unmappable in km; measured in days. A 3-day surface journey can take 3 weeks underground on return.

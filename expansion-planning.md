## Part 1: Full Lore Summary

### Setting

The game is titled **"Echoes of Iron and Aether"** (also referred to as "Iron & Blood" in the design documents). It is a **dieselpunk/crystal-punk** world split between two rival mega-nations locked in a cold war following a cataclysmic Great War.

**Terra** is an industrial nation powered by coal, diesel, and iron. It is militaristic, bureaucratic, and smog-choked. Its cities are vast Hive-like mega-structures, its people pragmatic survivors. Terra's culture prizes loyalty, endurance, and mechanical ingenuity.

**Aetheria** is a crystal-powered nation that harnesses Aetherium — a luminescent mineral that acts as a nearly inexhaustible energy source. Aetherian cities float, gleam, and hum with arcane-technological energy. Their culture prizes precision, scholarship, and a carefully maintained myth of civilization. Aetherium, however, corrupts: prolonged exposure without shielding causes mutation and madness.

Between them lies the **Gray Wastes** — the shattered no-man's-land from the Great War, littered with spent Aetherium shards, rusted machinery, and the people too stubborn or unlucky to belong to either nation. The Gray Wastes have their own ecology of scavengers, criminals, and survivors. Aetherium concentrations there are highest in the world.

Other regions include the **Deep Iron** (underground territories inhabited by a dwarven-analogue culture of engineers and miners), the **Iron Monastery** (a neutral religious/philosophical order that worships neither crystal nor iron, but the balance between), the **Machine Communion** (a techno-cult that believes machines are becoming conscious), and **The Hollow** (a region of extreme Aetherium corruption where reality itself becomes unstable — home to the game's most enigmatic figures).

### History

The **Great War** consumed both nations. Neither won. The war ended with the **Armistice of Silence**, a formal ceasefire that ended open warfare but resolved nothing. Both nations maintain massive standing armies, spy networks, and proxy conflicts. Trade between them is technically legal but deeply politically charged, conducted through the Gray Wastes's gray markets.

The Armistice includes a mechanical provision — the **Nightly Ceasefire** — under which active combat is formally suspended during certain hours. Both sides honor it just enough to call it operative.

The central narrative threat is **Project Lazarus**: a secret research program (origin deliberately ambiguous — possibly Terra, possibly rogue elements, possibly Machine Communion) developing necrotic technology using corrupted Aetherium, called **Lazarus Steel**. It reanimates dead soldiers, creating **Lazarus Subjects** — shambling, corrosive undead warriors. **Lazarus Sites** are the secret labs where this research continues. The Lazarus threat is what connects the game's factions: every faction has a stake in stopping (or controlling) Project Lazarus, but none can agree on how, or who is responsible.

**Aetherium** is the world's central resource and central problem. It powers everything in Aetheria. It can be traded, smuggled, weaponized, and refined. It also corrupts all living things with prolonged exposure. The **Abominations** — the game's most dangerous enemy type — are humans and animals fully consumed by Aetherium exposure. The Gray Wastes produce them constantly.

### Factions (9 Total)

The game has **nine factions**, each with a distinct ideology, visual identity, and relationship to the other factions. Each faction contributes six playable classes, for a total of 54 classes.

**1. Terra** — The industrial military state. Classes: Vanguard (heavy frontline melee), Heavy Engineer (grenade launchers and combat gauntlets), Field Engineer (SCAMP turret deployer), Ironclad (armored tank), Field Medic (syringe launcher healer), Rifleman (long-range precision). Terra equipment is tagged `[T]`. Terra currency is **Iron Scraps**.

**2. Aetheria** — The crystal nation. Classes: Aether Knight, Aether Sage, Crystal Weaver, Aether Medic, Void Walker, Storm Caller. Aetheria equipment tagged `[A]`. Aetheria currency is **Aetherium Shards**.

**3. Gray Wastes** — The outlaw nation. Classes: Scavenger, Wasteland Shaman, Iron Reaper, Gray Phantom, Bone Doctor, Wraith Blade. Equipment tagged `[G]` (Gray). Gray Wastes characters use both currencies and deal in the exchange rate between them.

**4. Deep Iron** — The underground engineers. Led by **Elder Thrum** and **Korvin**. Six classes focused on heavy industry and underground warfare. Equipment tagged faction-neutral in many cases.

**5. Iron Monastery** — The neutral order. Led by **Abbot Solan** and **Brother Kell**. Six classes blending physical discipline with meditative ability. The Monastery trades with everyone and is universally tolerated — making it the best intelligence network in the world.

**6. Machine Communion** — The techno-cult. Led by **Architect Zane** and **Sister Chroma**. Believes machines are developing consciousness and seeks to accelerate that process. Their six classes are the most mechanically unconventional, blending technology with fanatical devotion.

**7. The Hollow** — The corrupted region faction. Led by **The Watcher** (identity deliberately obscured). Six classes built around Aetherium corruption as a power source rather than a hazard. Feared by all other factions.

**8. Project Lazarus** (enemy faction) — Not a playable faction but a fully designed enemy roster with six enemy class types: basic Lazarus Subjects, armored variants, elite Lazarus Commanders, and the Abbot-class (a corrupted Iron Monastery defector template).

**9. Abominations** (enemy faction) — Six creature types: Rock-Shell Crab, Iron-Beak Vulture, and four others of increasing Aetherium corruption severity. Special loot sources for rare Lazarus and crystal materials.

### Named Characters (Book of Faces)

The lore contains detailed profiles for 21+ named characters:

**Terra:** Korr Vane (veteran commander, tactical pragmatist, deeply scarred by the war), Helen Stone (field medic, the faction's moral center), Marcus Thorn (heavy engineer, dark-humored, fatalistic), Gideon Moss (field engineer, cheerful inventor of SCAMP, the turret AI), Lena Vance (rifleman, methodical, isolationist), Aris Thorne (vanguard, idealistic, will get disillusioned).

**Aetheria:** Elara Vance (aether knight, disciplined but questioning), Darius Kane (aether sage, brilliant and arrogant), Celia Morn (crystal weaver, artist-scientist), Soren Vale (void walker, eroded by Hollow exposure), Kira Vance (Elara's younger sibling, untested).

**Gray Wastes:** Morlak (de facto leader, opportunist with a code), Grimka (enforcer, believes in nothing), Vespa (information broker, believes in everything — for a price).

**Deep Iron:** Elder Thrum (the last keeper of pre-war engineering traditions), Korvin (practical, bridges old and new).

**Iron Monastery:** Abbot Solan (pacifist who fears he will have to choose sides), Brother Kell (has already chosen, privately).

**Machine Communion:** Architect Zane (genuinely believes the machines are waking up), Sister Chroma (the only one who has heard one speak).

**The Hollow:** The Watcher (present in every faction's rumor mill; no confirmed identity, possibly multiple people).

### Themes

The lore returns repeatedly to a core set of thematic tensions:

- **Industrial civilization vs. natural/crystal forces** — Terra's diesel and iron vs. Aetheria's Aetherium, neither sustainable, both corrupt in their own way.
- **The aftermath of total war** — No faction is unmarked by the Great War. Every character carries it. The world is a wound that never closed.
- **Resource as addiction** — Aetherium is the world's fuel and its slow poison. Societies are structurally dependent on something that destroys them.
- **Moral ambiguity at scale** — The lore explicitly forbids easy villains. Terra does terrible things for understandable reasons. Aetheria does refined things that are also terrible. The Gray Wastes are violent and also the most honest.
- **The cost of survival** — A recurring motif across all character profiles: survival is not neutral. Every alive character made choices to stay alive that haunt them.

### Tone

Grimdark dieselpunk. The world is broken and the people in it know it. The writing is terse, world-worn, and sardonic rather than melodramatic. The **Book of Truths** (a lore section of sardonic aphorisms attributed to unnamed veterans) captures the register well: cynical, funny, and quietly devastating. NPC dialogue is written in short bursts, reflecting characters who don't have the luxury of speeches.

### Combat System (as designed)

The lore specifies a **turn-based tactical RPG** combat system with the following parameters:

**Statistics:** ATK, DEF, INT, HP, SPD (initiative), ACC (accuracy), CRIT (crit chance), DODGE.

**Damage formula:** Base damage = (ATK × modifier) − DEF ± random spread. Critical hits apply a multiplier (typically ×1.5–×3.0 depending on weapon). Dodge checks occur before damage resolves.

**Status effects (20+ designed):** Burn (DoT, fire), Bleed (DoT, physical), Poison (DoT, chemical), Stun (skip turn), Freeze (ATK reduction + slow), Slow (SPD reduction), Root (no movement), Blind (ACC penalty), Silence (no abilities), Confuse (random targeting), Fear (forced retreat), Charm (fights for enemy), Curse (vulnerability amplifier), Necrosis (Lazarus-specific progressive DoT), Aether Burn (Aetherium-specific), Crystal Shatter (armor penetration), and several faction-specific variants.

**The Armistice Mechanic:** Combat is formally suspended during the nightly ceasefire window. The game-time system should enforce this — enemies stand down, hostile actions return a specific refusal message rather than initiating combat.

### Weapon System (v7.0 Modular)

Weapons are assembled from three parts: **Frame** (determines weapon type and base stats), **Mechanism** (determines attack pattern), **Enhancement** (adds special effects). Nine weapon types with 4,337 total part combinations. Named synergy weapons emerge from specific combinations — e.g., "Storm Dancer" (Counterweight + Monomolecular + Electrified) grants +1 ATK per kill stacking to 3; "Grave Walker" (Two-Handed + Lazarus Steel + Necrotic) gives undead -30% damage dealt.

Items have **rarity tiers T1–T10** (Broken through Transcendent) with defined buy/sell/craft price scaling and drop rates by source type. Faction affiliation tags `[T]`, `[A]`, `[U]` (universal), `[G]` (Gray Wastes), `[X]` (exotic) determine where items can be purchased or traded.

### Dual Currency System

**Iron Scraps** are Terra's currency. **Aetherium Shards** are Aetheria's. Both circulate everywhere, but at different exchange rates depending on territory. The Gray Wastes operate a gray-market exchange. Vendor pricing is denominated in the dominant currency of the location.

### Dialogue System (designed)

A **Morrowind-style keyword dialogue system**: NPCs have a greeting and a set of **topics** (keywords). Players click or type a keyword to hear the NPC's response on that topic. NPC **disposition** (0–100) affects the depth and candor of responses. **Faction standing** also modifies dialogue options — a player with high Terra standing hears different responses from Terra NPCs than one who has been working against them.

### Special Class Mechanics

Several classes have mechanics that go significantly beyond basic attack/ability patterns:

**Field Engineer — SCAMP Turret:** A deployable persistent entity that auto-attacks each combat turn. The turret has its own HP, escalates through Mk.I–Mk.IV variants, and can be commanded (Focus Fire, Suppressing Fire, Overcharge). It persists between fights until destroyed.

**Field Medic — Canister System:** The Syringe Launcher uses swappable **Canisters** that define the active payload: Heal (restore HP), Buff (+ATK/DEF), Antidote (cleanse DoT), Stim (+SPD/CRIT), Adrenaline (prevent death once). The player switches active canister between rounds, creating resource management tension.

**Iron Apothecary — Payload System:** The Syringe Spear delivers melee attacks that inject **Payloads**: Necrosis (stacking Lazarus-type DoT), Plague (spreads between enemies), Venom (deep poison). Multiple payloads can stack with interaction effects.

**Heavy Engineer:** Grenade launcher (area denial, Mortar Barrage for multi-target), Combat Gauntlets (Iron Fist close-range burst), armor plating (passive DEF scaling with level).

**Ironclad:** Primary tank. Shield mechanics (blocking reducing incoming damage by a percentage), Fortress Stance, Taunt (forces enemies to target this unit). Highest HP and DEF in the game.

## Part 2: Current Engine & Systems

### Architecture

The engine is built in **Rust** using **Bevy ECS**, compiled to **WASM** for browser delivery. The architecture centers on a deterministic replay model: all game state is a pure function of a fixed RNG seed plus an immutable event log. This gives the engine **full time-travel / rewind capability** for free — rewinding to tick N means clearing all entities, re-bootstrapping the world, and replaying the event log to tick N.

The player interacts via **text commands** (`attack`, `go north`, `talk innkeeper`, etc.) plus **context action buttons** surfaced by the engine after each command. There is no separate `chronos-ui/` directory — the UI is likely a web frontend consuming the WASM module.

### ECS Components Implemented

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| `Health` | `current` and `max` HP |
| `Stats` | `attack`, `defense`, `intelligence` (3 stats only) |
| `ActiveEffects` | Container for status effects; pruned each tick |
| `Experience` | `xp` and `level` |
| `Wallet` | Single `gold` integer |
| `Position` | `room_id` reference |
| `Inventory` / `InInventory` | Item ownership tracking |
| `QuestLog` | Quest state entries |
| `Identity` | `name` and `class_id` |
| `Controllable` | Player entity marker |
| `Enemy` | Enemy entity marker |
| `ItemBlueprint` | Item instance reference |
| `GameTime` | In-game minutes; `skip_to_dawn()` for Rest |
| `Victory` | Win condition marker |

### Status Effects Implemented

Only **4** effect kinds are implemented: `Poison`, `Burn`, `Bleed` (all damage-over-time), and `DefenseUp` (self-buff). The engine architecture handles all four through a single `ActiveEffects` component with tick-based expiry — adding new effect kinds is a matter of extending the `EffectKind` enum and the `poison::tick_all_effects` handler.

### Systems Implemented

**Combat (`combat.rs`):** Turn-based single exchange per command. Player strikes first (ATK − DEF ± 2 RNG spread, min 1). Enemy retaliates using a data-driven **tactic system**: `BasicAttack`, `HeavyAttack { multiplier }`, or `ApplyEffect { kind, damage, duration }`. Tactic selection is rule-based with conditions: `Always`, `HpBelow { threshold }`, `PlayerHpAbove { threshold }`, `PlayerEffectAbsent { kind }`. XP and gold rewards on kill. Level-up: +1 ATK, +1 DEF, +5 max HP. Kill-count quest tracking fires after each kill.

**Dialogue (`dialogue.rs`):** `talk <npc>` returns greeting and available topic list. `ask <npc> <topic>` fuzzy-matches keyword and returns response. Context action buttons surface all available topics. No disposition system. No faction standing modifier. Vendor and rest-provider flags are respected. Quest availability shown at greeting.

**Quest (`quest.rs`):** `accept <quest_id>` adds to QuestLog. Kill-count objective is the only implemented type. `quests` command displays active and completed quests. Victory condition checks if all quests complete.

**Movement (`movement.rs`):** Cardinal direction navigation (n/s/e/w). `look` describes current room with exits, items, enemies, NPCs.

**Interaction (`interaction.rs`):** `take`, `drop`, `use`, `examine`, `inventory`. Items have `use_effect` attributes for consumables.

**Ability (`ability.rs`):** `use <ability>` executes class abilities from the class definition: `base_damage`, optional `hit_count`, optional `applies_effect` with `effect_damage` and `effect_duration`.

**Shop (`shop.rs`):** `shop <npc>` lists wares; `buy <npc> <item>` deducts gold and moves item to inventory.

**Time (`advance_game_time`):** Each action has a defined time cost (Move = 15 min, Attack = 1, Talk = 5, Rest skips to dawn). `GameTime` is a resource updated after each event.

**Rewind (`rewind_to_tick`):** Full deterministic rewind implemented; clears entities, re-bootstraps, replays event log.

**Save/Load:** `snapshot()` serializes full game state to JSON DTO; `load_from_snapshot()` restores via replay.

### Current World Content

The active world is **"Millbrook"**, a medieval fantasy town — entirely disconnected from the Iron & Blood lore. It exists as a functional demonstration of the engine's capabilities.

**Rooms (16):** town_square, inn, tavern, smithy, guild_hall, market, guard_house, mayor's_office, town_walls, forest, dark_forest, grove, river, dam, ancient_ruins, obsidian_dungeon.

**Enemies:** goblin (forest ×3), orc (dark_forest), skeleton (obsidian_dungeon), wraith (ancient_ruins).

**NPCs:** innkeeper (rest provider), blacksmith (vendor), guild_master (quest giver), aldwyn (vendor), captain_bren, brin.

**Quests:** goblin_hunt, orc_bounty, ruins_investigation, skeleton_purge (all kill-count objectives).

**Items:** iron_sword, leather_armor, health_potion, greater_health_potion, healing_herbs, iron_tonic, strength_elixir, scholars_draught, ancient_amulet, velian_tome.

**Player Classes:** fighter (120 HP, 9 ATK, 8 DEF), alchemist (85 HP, 8 ATK, 5 DEF, 8 INT), hunter, medica, plague_knight.

## Part 4: Expansion Tracks

The work ahead splits cleanly into two independent tracks. **Engine changes** require a Rust/TypeScript developer touching `chronos-core`, `chronos-wasm`, or the frontend. **Content changes** require a game designer editing JSON files under `worlds/` — no compilation, no code review. Where a feature needs both, it is listed in each track with an explicit dependency note.

The two tracks can proceed in parallel. Content work that has no engine prerequisites can start immediately. Content work that is gated on engine changes is called out clearly below.

---

### Engine Track

*All items in this track require code changes. Hand this list to a developer.*

#### E-1 — Dual Currency Wallet `[ENGINE]`
Change `Wallet` from `gold: i32` to `iron_scraps: i32` and `aetherium_shards: i32`. Update every system that reads or writes gold (`shop.rs`, `combat.rs` kill rewards, `snapshot` serialization). This is a small change with wide data implications — do it before any content work touches vendor pricing or loot rewards.

#### E-2 — Rarity Field in ItemTemplate `[ENGINE]`
Add `rarity: u8` (1–10) to the `ItemTemplate` struct and JSON schema. Existing items default to T3. Required before loot tables or tiered vendor pricing can be implemented. Content can begin tagging item files immediately after this ships.

#### E-3 — Faction Standing Component `[ENGINE]`
Add `FactionStanding { standings: HashMap<String, i32> }` to the player entity. Expose a `modify_faction_standing(faction_id, delta)` helper. Update `dialogue.rs` and `quest.rs` to read standings when evaluating topic availability and quest rewards. This unblocks all faction-loyalty quests and conditional NPC dialogue.

#### E-4 — Additional Status Effects `[ENGINE]`
Extend `EffectKind` with: `Stun` (skip turn), `Freeze` (ATK reduction + slow), `Slow` (SPD reduction), `Root` (no movement), `Blind` (ACC penalty), `Silence` (no abilities), `Necrosis` (Lazarus-specific stacking DoT), `AetherBurn` (Aetherium-specific DoT), `CrystalShatter` (armor penetration). The existing tick/apply/expire architecture in `active_effects.rs` handles all of these — only the enum variants and their behavior branches in `tick_all_effects` need to be added.

#### E-5 — Armistice Ceasefire Combat Gate `[ENGINE]`
Add a check at the top of `process_attack` in `combat.rs` that reads `GameTime` and returns a narrative refusal message if the current in-game time falls within the ceasefire window. The window bounds (e.g. 00:00–06:00) should be a configurable field in the world manifest so content can set it per-world without another code change.

#### E-6 — SPD / ACC / CRIT / DODGE Stats `[ENGINE]`
Extend the `Stats` component with `speed: i32`, `accuracy: i32`, `crit_chance: f32`, `dodge: f32`. Update `process_attack` to: (1) roll accuracy vs. defender's dodge before computing damage, (2) roll crit on hit and apply the class-defined crit multiplier. Enemy and class JSON schemas need corresponding new fields; content can populate them once this ships.

#### E-7 — Targeting Choice in Combat `[ENGINE]`
Add `target_name: Option<String>` to the `Attack` event. When `None`, preserve current behavior (first live enemy in room). When `Some`, select the named enemy. Surface available targets as context action buttons after a `look` or when multiple enemies are present.

#### E-8 — SCAMP Turret (Player-Owned Persistent Entity) `[ENGINE]`
Add `PlayerOwnedEntity` and `Turret { hp, max_hp, mk_level: u8 }` ECS components. Bootstrap spawns no turrets. The Field Engineer's `deploy_scamp` ability spawns a turret entity in the current room. Combat resolution in `combat.rs` must iterate player-owned entities and fire their attacks each tick. Add `upgrade_scamp` and `command_scamp { mode }` event types for Mk escalation and Fire/Suppressing/Overcharge modes. Turret state persists in snapshots.

#### E-9 — Active Loadout / Canister-Payload System `[ENGINE]`
Add `ActiveLoadout { slots: Vec<String> }` to the player entity. Add `SwapLoadout { slot_index, item_id }` event. Field Medic and Iron Apothecary ability resolution reads the active loadout slot rather than a fixed ability definition. The `Plague` payload needs a spread-to-adjacent-enemies pass after it applies — this is the only behavior that requires iterating all enemies in the room.

#### E-10 — Additional Quest Objective Types `[ENGINE]`
Extend `QuestObjective` enum beyond `KillCount` to support `FetchItem { item_id, count }` and `VisitRoom { room_id }`. `FetchItem` resolves when the player has the required items in inventory at turn-in; `VisitRoom` fires when `movement.rs` places the player in the target room. These two types cover the majority of narrative quest needs.

#### E-11 — Loot Generation on Kill `[ENGINE]`
Add a `loot_table: Vec<{ item_id, weight, min_rarity }>` field to the enemy class JSON schema. After a kill in `combat.rs`, roll the loot table and spawn the result into the current room (or directly into inventory, configurable per-world). Replace the fixed gold reward with currency drops drawn from the same table.

#### E-12 — Modular Weapon Crafting `[ENGINE]`
Add `CraftWeapon { frame, mechanism, enhancement }` event type.
